The line

From a feed URL to a fix, in four stops.

No setup. We track GTFS feeds across the United States and Canada. Find yours and read the grade.

STOP 01

We fetch your published feed

The same GTFS (opens in a new tab) file trip planners read. Once a day, politely, straight from the URL you already publish.

STOP 02

We run the canonical validator

The MobilityData GTFS validator (opens in a new tab) the apps and states use. We score on top of it; we don't reinvent the rules.

STOP 03

We translate it to plain language

A letter grade and four category scores: correctness, freshness, rider experience, and realtime. Every finding is rewritten as a plain-language fix with an effort hint, never a failure.

STOP 04

You get the three things to do first

Ordered by what hurts riders most. An expired feed comes before a cosmetic gap. Start at the top; one fix is a real win.

A new obligation

Ready for your NTD certification?

Since Report Year 2023, every NTD (opens in a new tab) reporter with fixed-route or deviated-fixed-route service has to publish a valid, current feed and certify it each year. Plenty of small agencies inherited that duty with no easy way to check where they stand.

Published

The link works

Your feed downloads from the public URL you report. That viable link is the first thing the FTA looks for.

Valid

No blocking errors

It clears the canonical validator, the same bar the apps and state programs apply.

Current

The calendar holds

Service data has not lapsed, so trip planners keep showing your agency to riders.

Each agency page reads these three for you and says, in plain words, whether the feed looks ready to certify. It is a heads-up, not an official determination; your annual D-10 certification (opens in a new tab) is the official one.
